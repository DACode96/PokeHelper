δ Delta Species

Pokémon with the wrong element type carry a δ symbol in the top right corner. A Metal-type Pikachu, a Dark-type Mewtwo. This rare mechanic only appeared in three sets: Delta Species, Legend Maker, and Holon Phantoms.

➤ Look for the δ symbol — a wrong type for that Pokémon = delta variant.

A gold stamp reading "STAFF" is given to tournament staff at official regional and international Pokémon Championships. These cards exist in very limited quantities and rarely surface. Any Pokémon card with a STAFF stamp is a top-tier collector's item.

➤ Gold/black stamp literally saying STAFF — never sell for a dollar.

Card Anatomy

What to check on every single card you pick up

1
Card Name

Top of the card. Is it a Pokémon, Trainer, or Energy? Check for suffixes: EX, GX, V, VMAX, ex, VSTAR.

2
HP Number

Top right. Higher HP generally = newer, stronger card. 300+ HP = modern era VMAX/ex.

3
Set Symbol + Number

Bottom right. e.g. "4/102". Star = promo. The set symbol identifies exactly which set it belongs to.

4
Copyright Year

Bottom of the card. 1995, 96, 98, 99 = Base Set era. Older year ≠ always more valuable.

5
Rarity Symbol

Next to the card number. ● = common, ◆ = uncommon, ★ = rare, ★★ = ultra rare, ★★★ = secret rare.

6
1st Edition / Stamp

Left side below the image. Small black stamp with "1" = 1st Edition. Gold "STAFF" stamp = tournament staff card.

7
Shadow on Frame

Does the image have a drop shadow on the right/bottom edge? No shadow = Shadowless = valuable first print.

8
Card Finish

Is the image shiny? Entire card rainbow? Gold border? Or plain paper? See the finish guide below.
